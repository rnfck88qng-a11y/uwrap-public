# Reviewer Prompt

Please review this as a skeptical systems/security reviewer.

I do not want praise. I want attack points.

## Questions

1. Which claims are genuinely novel?
2. Which claims are already covered by existing provenance, attestation, or policy systems?
3. Where does the paper overclaim bounded proofs into platform claims?
4. Which terms are still too vague to survive criticism?
5. Which one or two ideas are strong enough to matter even if the rest of the stack never ships?
6. Where does the doctrine survive scrutiny better than the architecture?
7. Where does the paper still carry accidental marketing language?
8. Which external citations are most necessary for credibility?

## Review posture

Assume the author is trying to stay honest.

Help make the paper harder to break.

## Important constraints

Do not evaluate it as a finished product launch.
Evaluate it as:
- a doctrine paper
- a bounded proof packet
- an open research agenda

## Requested outputs

Please provide:

- strongest surviving claims
- weakest claims
- likely criticisms
- terminology problems
- one-paragraph verdict
- concrete sentence rewrites where the paper drifts above its evidence
