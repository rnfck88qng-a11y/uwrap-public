Hi Demis — your recent 20VC conversation on AGI scale and bottlenecks pushed me to write this.

I think there is one bottleneck still missing from the mainstream AGI discussion: governability after distribution.

Once capable AI is cheap, local, and forkable, safety cannot depend only on provider moderation, hosted APIs, or policy after the fact. It needs a bounded execution layer where AI may propose, actions are receipted, contradictions remain visible, and only humans accept canonical state.

I’ve written a short working paper called The Sovereign Acceptance Runtime. It is not a finished-platform claim; it is a doctrine paper on the missing governance layer for portable AI.

I’d value one thing only: your reaction to whether this is a real missing bottleneck, or whether you think current safety thinking already covers it.
