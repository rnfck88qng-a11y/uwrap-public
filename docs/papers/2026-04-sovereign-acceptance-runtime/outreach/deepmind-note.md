I’m sharing a short working paper because I think it identifies a gap between current AI safety work and the emerging distribution model of AI systems.

The thesis is simple: once capable models are cheap, local, and forkable, provider safeguards are necessary but not sufficient. Governance has to move to the execution boundary.

The paper proposes a bounded layer called the Sovereign Acceptance Runtime:
- AI may propose
- actions must be receipted
- contradictions remain visible
- projection is not truth
- only humans accept canonical state

This is deliberately written as a doctrine paper with bounded proofs and explicit open problems, not as a finished-platform claim.

If useful, I’d value one hard reaction: does this identify a real missing layer, or do you think current safety and governance work already covers it?
