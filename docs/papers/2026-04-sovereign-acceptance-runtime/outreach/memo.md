# A Missing AGI Bottleneck: Governability After Distribution

## Thesis
Once capable AI runs locally, cheaply, and outside centralized provider control, safety cannot rely only on model moderation. It needs a bounded execution layer where AI may propose, actions are receipted, contradictions remain visible, and only humans accept canonical state.

## Why now
- capable models are increasingly portable and edge-capable
- provider-level moderation is therefore necessary but insufficient
- governance needs an execution-boundary complement

## Contribution
This paper proposes four linked ideas:
- Sovereign Acceptance as a runtime discipline
- Tri-State Truth as an honesty protocol
- receipt-driven governance as a proof grammar
- STRONG/WEAK guard separation as a bounded execution pattern

## What it does not claim
- not a finished platform
- not solved AI governance
- not a reconciled full stack

## Ask
The question is simple:
does this identify a genuine missing layer in the AGI conversation?
