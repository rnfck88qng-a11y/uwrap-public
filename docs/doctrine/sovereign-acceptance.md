# Sovereign Acceptance

A **Sovereign Acceptance Runtime** is a bounded execution layer in which AI may propose but may not unilaterally declare canonical truth.

## Core meaning

The runtime exists to ensure that:
- AI may propose
- actions are bounded
- evidence is reviewable
- contradiction remains visible
- only humans accept canonical state

## Why it exists

As models become cheaper, more portable, and increasingly local, governance cannot depend only on centralized providers or after-the-fact dashboards.

The control problem moves to the execution boundary.

## Public rule

AI may propose.  
Humans alone declare CANON.

## Projection is not truth

Dashboards and interfaces are projection surfaces.
They do not own truth.
They do not define canonical state.
They render governed state produced elsewhere.

## Paper discipline

Sovereign Acceptance is the doctrine.
The current stack is not yet a completed realization of that doctrine.
